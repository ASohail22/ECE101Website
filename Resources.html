    Math Resources

    Calculus Resources:

    Having a solid fundamental in mathematics is important as an ECE student. Calculus is one of the first classes that you take as a first-year student. Some good math resources for calculus can be found on YouTube! Professor Leonard has in-depth videos covering Calculus I-III topics, along with Linear Algebra and Ordinary Differential Equations once you take Math for Engineering Analysis!

    Physics Resources

    Electromagnetic Resources

    First year ECE students have to take Introductory Mechanics and Intro to Electricity and Magnetism. These physics concepts, especially Electricity and Magnetism will come back in future classes. Michel Van Biezan has a lot of youtube videos that do practice problems explaining these concepts, and Khan Academy has a lot of good general videos explaining key physics concepts.

    Programming Resources

    Almost every class in your ECE curriculum will require you to do some sort of programming, whether it be in Arduino, C++, or MATLAB. A good resource for C++ and Java (if you are a CS minor) will be The New Boston's Youtube videos.

    In the ECE101 class, you will be programming an Arduino. The Arduino website is an excellent resource for sample code troubleshooting help. If you are looking for more in-depth help with using the Arduino with various components, such as a joystick, or LCD, Paul McWhorter's YouTube channel has a ton of great videos.

    The second ECE class you will take your freshman year is Intro to Digital Systems. CompArchIllinois

    has a few videos talking about topics that will be covered in the class such as 2's complement, along with verilog code examples.
